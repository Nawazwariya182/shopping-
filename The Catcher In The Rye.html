"The Catcher in the Rye" is a novel written by J.D. Salinger, first published in 1951. The novel tells the story of Holden Caulfield, a teenage boy who has been expelled from his prep school and is struggling with the transition to adulthood. Holden is a complex character who is often portrayed as being disillusioned with society, cynical about the world around him, and struggling with mental health issues. Throughout the novel, Holden struggles to find a sense of purpose or meaning in his life, and he often feels alienated from those around him.
